# Contributor Covenant Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

We as members, contributors, and maintainers pledge to make participation in the Tainted Grail Modding Editor and SDK community a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, visible or invisible disability, ethnicity, sex characteristics, gender identity and expression, level of experience, education, socio-economic status, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

We pledge to act and interact in ways that contribute to an open, welcoming, diverse, inclusive, and healthy community.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment include:

- demonstrating empathy and kindness;
- respecting differing opinions, viewpoints, and experiences;
- giving and gracefully accepting constructive technical feedback;
- discussing evidence, code, and behavior rather than attacking people;
- accepting responsibility, correcting mistakes, and learning from them;
- respecting boundaries around proprietary game data, security reports, and personal information;
- focusing on the long-term safety and health of the community and project.

Examples of unacceptable behavior include:

- sexualized language or imagery and sexual attention or advances;
- trolling, insults, derogatory comments, or personal and political attacks;
- public or private harassment;
- publishing another person's private information without permission;
- threats, intimidation, stalking, or sustained disruption;
- pressuring contributors to disclose proprietary, private, or security-sensitive information;
- using project spaces to distribute malware, stolen material, pirated assets, or abusive tools;
- retaliation against people who raise good-faith safety, conduct, or quality concerns;
- other conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional community.

## Technical disagreement

Strong technical disagreement is allowed. It must remain respectful and evidence-based.

Reviewers and contributors should:

- state the risk or requirement clearly;
- provide reproducible examples when possible;
- distinguish required changes from optional preferences;
- avoid sarcasm, gatekeeping, and status-based arguments;
- accept a maintainer's documented final decision after reasonable discussion.

## Enforcement responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying and enforcing these standards. They may remove, edit, hide, lock, or reject comments, commits, issues, pull requests, documentation, or other contributions that violate this Code of Conduct.

Maintainers will communicate reasons for moderation decisions when doing so is appropriate and safe.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies in all project spaces, including:

- repository issues, pull requests, reviews, and discussions;
- project documentation and community chat spaces;
- events or calls organised for this project;
- public spaces where someone is officially representing the project.

## Reporting

For conduct occurring on GitHub, use GitHub's report, block, and abuse-reporting controls when applicable.

For repository-specific moderation, open a minimal issue labelled as a conduct or moderation concern **without including private or sensitive details**. A maintainer will arrange an appropriate private channel when needed.

Do not use a public issue for security vulnerabilities. Follow [SECURITY.md](SECURITY.md).

All good-faith reports will be reviewed as promptly and fairly as project capacity allows. Maintainers must protect the privacy and safety of reporters and affected people.

## Enforcement guidelines

Maintainers will use the following impact ladder, considering context, severity, intent, history, and community safety.

### 1. Correction

**Impact:** Unprofessional or unwelcome behavior with limited community impact.

**Response:** A private or public correction explaining the problem and expected behavior. An apology or edited comment may be requested.

### 2. Warning

**Impact:** A significant incident or repeated lower-level behavior.

**Response:** A formal warning and conditions for continued participation. Contact restrictions or temporary removal from a discussion may be imposed.

### 3. Temporary restriction

**Impact:** Serious or sustained violation of community standards.

**Response:** Temporary restriction from project interaction, review, or contribution for a defined period.

### 4. Permanent ban

**Impact:** A pattern of severe violations, harassment, threats, malicious activity, or refusal to comply with prior enforcement.

**Response:** Permanent exclusion from project spaces under maintainer control.

## Appeals and conflicts of interest

A person affected by an enforcement action may request reconsideration by a maintainer who was not directly involved when another maintainer is available.

Maintainers must disclose conflicts of interest and recuse themselves when practical.
